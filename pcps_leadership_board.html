Governing framework

Policy flows down.
Accountability flows up.

Each level of this framework creates obligations for the level below it. Fulfilling the building-level obligation — a teacher completing a form — simultaneously satisfies every obligation above it.

Federal Law
FERPA · ESSA · IDEA Part B · Title I · Title III
🔒FERPA (20 U.S.C. § 1232g) governs student data access — the role-gated architecture of this system is a direct compliance mechanism
📊ESSA requires disaggregated public reporting of student outcomes at the division level
♿IDEA Part B extends reading plan requirements to students with IEPs who do not meet reading benchmarks
💼Title I and Title III require documented parent engagement and literacy intervention as conditions of federal funding
📅 Federal fiscal year: Oct 1 – Sep 30 · Annual performance report due October
Virginia Law
Virginia Literacy Act · § 22.1-253.13:1 G · VDOE Guidance
📋Effective SY 2025–26, Student Reading Plans are mandatory for every K–8 student who fails or scores Basic on the Reading SOL or scores high-risk on the VALLSS screener
👩‍🏫Each plan must be co-owned by a licensed reading specialist and reference a state board-approved intervention program
👨‍👩‍👧A documented parent participation offer is required for every plan — any family that declines must do so in writing, and that record must be retained
📅 Plans active by start of year · SOL testing window: April · VDOE division compliance report: end of year

VLA compliance fields

Five required actions.
Every student. Every year.

The Virginia Literacy Act, FERPA, and federal grant requirements define five actions the division must take and document for every student on a reading plan. Each field in the system corresponds directly to one of those requirements.

📋
Reading plan created
Documents that the division has identified the student and initiated a formal response. This is the foundational VLA compliance record. A student without a plan is tracked as "not yet assigned" — a status that generates an alert and a defined next step rather than a gap in the record.
VLA division reportMonthly board updateTitle I documentation
👩‍🏫
Reading specialist assigned and confirmed
§ 22.1-253.13:1 G requires a licensed reading specialist to co-develop, oversee, and monitor every Student Reading Plan. Specialist assignment is handled automatically by the system based on school and grade band configuration. Co-ownership is confirmed and logged — a plan is not complete until this step is documented.
VLA statutory requirementVDOE compliance record
✉️
Parent participation offered and letter distributed
The VLA requires that divisions offer families the opportunity to participate in plan development. The parent letter documents this offer. Distribution is confirmed by the teacher through a single-field update, which closes this field in the compliance record and opens the parent response window.
VLA participation requirementTitle I family engagementDivision compliance rate
🤝
Parent response documented
Each response status — accepted, declined, or pending — has a defined downstream action. Accepted families are recorded as engaged partners. Pending families receive a follow-up within the district's stated response window. Declined families trigger Field 5. This field is what makes the compliance record auditable rather than administrative.
VLA audit trailTitle I family engagementQuarterly board report
🗂️
Written refusal on file (when applicable)
When a family declines intervention services, the VLA requires written documentation retained by the school. The district provides a standard refusal form. Once signed, uploaded to the school's secure folder, and confirmed by the data coordinator, this field is closed. The record documents both the family's decision and the division's prior offer of services.

✅

When all five fields are confirmed for a student, Petersburg holds a complete, FERPA-compliant compliance record that satisfies the Virginia Literacy Act, supports Title I reporting, and can be exported for a board meeting, a state compliance review, or a federal grant renewal — from the district view, in under one minute.

Leadership Q&A

Prepared responses for
governance and oversight audiences.

The following addresses questions commonly raised by board members, grant officers, and state reviewers about literacy compliance infrastructure.

🔍
How does the division ensure every identified student receives a reading plan?
Students are identified automatically from screener and assessment data uploaded by the data coordinator. Every identified student enters the plan queue and generates an alert if a plan has not been initiated within the expected window. No manual tracking is required to maintain completeness.
👩‍🏫
How is reading specialist co-ownership documented across all plans?
Specialist assignment is applied automatically based on a school and grade band configuration table. Confirmation of co-ownership is logged as Field 2 in each student's compliance record. A plan does not reach complete status until the specialist has confirmed their assignment in the system.
👨‍👩‍👧
What is the process when a family declines intervention services?
The teacher logs the declined status. A district-standard written refusal form is signed by the family, uploaded to the school's secure folder, and confirmed by the data coordinator. Field 5 remains open until documentation is on file. The record documents both the family's decision and the division's prior offer of services.
📋
What compliance evidence can the division produce on request?
A complete, date-stamped record for every student — plan creation, specialist assignment, letter distributed, parent response, and refusal documentation where applicable. The full record is exportable from the district view in under one minute and is current as of the most recent data upload.
💼
How does this system support Title I and grant renewal documentation?
Parent engagement data, literacy intervention records, and compliance rates are logged continuously in the system. At Title I APR and grant renewal deadlines, the data is already structured and available — no manual aggregation is required from staff.
🔑
How does the division maintain continuity if key personnel change?
All credentials, processes, and documentation transfer to the district's technology lead at Day 21. Role-specific quick-start guides and recorded orientations are included in the handoff package. Any new staff member in any role reaches full operational proficiency in approximately 30 minutes using documentation alone.
