The governing framework

Every field, every report, every parent letter traces back to a statute or standard. This is not a design choice — it is the law, translated into infrastructure.

Federal Law
FERPA · ESSA · IDEA · Title I / Title III
FERPA governs who may see student data — the role-gated architecture is a direct compliance mechanism. ESSA defines public reporting and disaggregated outcome requirements. IDEA requires reading plans even for IEP students who fail the Reading SOL. Title I and III govern how federal dollars support intervention services.
20 U.S.C. § 1232gESSAIDEA Part BTitle I · Title III
Virginia Law
Virginia Literacy Act · VDOE Guidance · VBOE Approved Programs
§ 22.1-253.13:1 G — SY 2025–26 is the first year K–8 Student Reading Plans are mandatory statewide. Students who fail or score Basic on the Reading SOL, or score high-risk on VALLSS, must have a plan co-owned by a licensed reading specialist. Parent participation must be offered and any refusal documented in writing. Every intervention must use a VBOE-approved program.
§ 22.1-253.13:1 GVALLSS screenerVBOE-approved programsVDOE parent letter templates
